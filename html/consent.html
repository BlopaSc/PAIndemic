What are some general things you should know about research studies?

You are being asked to take part in a research study. Your participation in this study is voluntary. What is the purpose of this study?

The purpose of the study is to determine if the use of plan recognition in an articial intelligence (AI) agent improves its cooperation with humans in the game Pandemic. You must be between 18 and 64 years to participate.

What will happen if you take part in the study?

If you agree to participate in this study, you will play one game of a browser-based implementation of the game Pandemic with an AI controlled player. Afterwards you will answer some questions about your previous experience with board games and your experience playing with the agent. If you enjoy your experience (and we hope you do), you can also play more often.

Risks

The risks of this study do not exceed normal computer use.

Benefits

There are no direct benefits to your participation in the research. The indirect benefits are that you may actually enjoy playing the game with the AI. For participating in this study you will receive no compensation.

Confidentiality

The information in the study records will be kept confidential to the full extent allowed by law. No personal or identifiable data will be stored. Gathered data will be stored securely on a secure computer at the researches computer. No reference will be made in oral or written reports which could link you to the study. After the conclusion of the study, the information we gathered will be released to the public to foster further research. Note that none of this information can be linked back to you personally.

What if you have questions about this study?

If you have questions at any time about the study itself or the procedures implemented in this study, you may contact the researcher, Pablo Sauma-Chacón by email: pablo.saumachacon@ucr.ac.cr
